2. What types of connectors can be used?

The FMC standard specifies a low-pin count (LPC) connector with 160 pins or high-pin count (HPC) connector with 400 pins. An LPC connector on mezzanine card side also fits into a HPC connector on baseboard side. Electrical signals are also compatible, as the LPC pinout is a reduced variant of the HPC specification. At the moment, there are two major manufacturers for the connectors on baseboard (female) and mezzanine card side (male), Samtec and Molex. The variants are listed in the table below.

3. What EEPROM can be used for FRU information storage?

In general, any serial EEPROM with I2C interface can be used. The most significant bits of the 7-bit I2C address should be 0b10100xx, where the two least significant bits are determined by the GA[1:0] pins of the FMC connector. Here is a list of commonly used chips we have seen on several FMC modules.

Manufacturer Part number Description Memory size
Microchip AT24C02D 2k I2C Serial EEPROM 256 x 8bit
Microchip 24AA64 64k I2C Serial EEPROM 8K x 8bit
Microchip 24LC64 64k I2C Serial EEPROM 8K x 8bit
STMicroelectronics M24C02 2k I2C Serial EEPROM 256 x 8bit
